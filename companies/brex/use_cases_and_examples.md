## DoorDash

DoorDash is a global delivery company. Brex's DoorDash case study says DoorDash had 23,000+ employees, operated in 30 countries, and used several corporate card/platform solutions before Brex. The finance team wanted one global platform for cards, employee reimbursements, proactive spend controls, and real-time visibility.

Products used:

- Corporate cards.
- Expense management.
- ERP integration.

End-user walkthrough:

1. DoorDash employee needs to travel or spend for work.
2. Brex policy and spend limits tell the employee what is allowed.
3. Employee uses a Brex card or submits reimbursement through the app.
4. Manager/finance gets visibility before or near the moment of spend.
5. Brex routes receipts, memos, and policy checks into the expense workflow.
6. Finance sees global spend in one place instead of reconciling multiple card programs later.
7. ERP integration helps the accounting team close faster.

Brex says reimbursements are paid in 4 days or less instead of weeks. DoorDash's team frames the key value as moving from "chase money after it is spent" to clarity on how money is being spent from the start.

Source: [DoorDash case study](https://www.brex.com/resources/customer/doordash).

## SeatGeek

SeatGeek is a ticketing marketplace and venue software company. Brex's case study says SeatGeek had over 700 employees and seven international offices. The old process relied heavily on reimbursements, manual card close, and delayed expense submission, which hurt finance visibility.

Products used:

- Expense management.
- Travel.
- Accounting automation.
- Corporate card controls.

End-user walkthrough:

1. Employee needs to book travel for an event, meeting, or live ticket experience.
2. Instead of booking outside policy and submitting reimbursement later, the employee uses Brex's travel/spend workflow.
3. Spend is tied to budgets/policies at the time of action.
4. Receipts, memos, and GL categorization are automated.
5. Finance sees spend in real time and avoids chasing missing documentation.
6. Month-end close is faster because the transaction already carries accounting context.

Public outcomes claimed by Brex:

- T&E costs reduced by 50% with only 15% less travel.
- 99% employee compliance.
- 99.7% of transactions auto-coded by Brex AI.
- Documentation compliance work reduced from 22 hours to 2 hours.
- Month-end close takes 15 minutes for this workflow.

Source: [SeatGeek case study](https://www.brex.com/resources/customer/seatgeek).

## Startup founder workflow

This is the original Brex wedge.

Problem:

- Startup has funding or fast revenue growth, but little operating history.
- Traditional banks/card issuers may require personal guarantees, low limits, or slow underwriting.
- Founder wants cards quickly for SaaS, travel, ads, cloud, recruiting, and operations.

Brex flow:

1. Startup applies.
2. Brex evaluates business factors such as revenue or dollars raised.
3. Company receives a card/account setup if approved.
4. Founder/admin issues virtual and physical cards.
5. Employees spend under controls.
6. Finance gets transaction visibility and accounting sync from day one.

The lesson: credit/float was a better acquisition hook than "expense management" alone. Once Brex owned card spend, it could expand into software.

## Enterprise finance workflow

Problem:

- Large company has many employees, teams, countries, entities, and vendors.
- Spend happens across cards, invoices, travel, reimbursements, and bank transfers.
- Finance only discovers problems after spend is completed.

Brex flow:

1. Finance maps entities, departments, users, roles, and budgets.
2. Policies are embedded into cards, travel, reimbursements, and AP.
3. Employees spend against pre-approved limits.
4. AI and rules collect receipts, generate memos, flag issues, and code expenses.
5. Managers review exceptions instead of every transaction.
6. Accounting exports cleaner records to ERP.
7. CFO/FP&A gets live budget and spend reporting.

The lesson: the high-value product is control before spend plus reconciliation after spend.

## What these examples tell us

Brex is not only selling "corporate cards." The recurring pattern is:

- Replace disconnected finance tools.
- Move controls earlier in the spend lifecycle.
- Reduce employee busywork.
- Reduce accounting cleanup.
- Produce a cleaner system of record for company spend.

This is exactly the part a stablecoin CFO stack would need to replicate if it wants to be more than a wallet and on/off-ramp layer.
