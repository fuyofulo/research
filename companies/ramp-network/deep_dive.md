## 1. What Ramp Network actually is (entity disambiguation)

Ramp Network — operated by the holding entity **Ramp Swaps Limited** (UK Companies House #11850124, incorporated 27 Feb 2019) and its subsidiaries — is a fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-fiat **on/off-ramp infrastructure provider**. It builds embedded SDK/widget rails that let other web3 products (wallets, dApps, games, browsers) sell crypto to end users denominated in 40+ fiat currencies, plus the inverse cash-out path. Since mid-2025 it has also shipped a consumer-facing **self-custodial multichain wallet** (passkey-based), pivoting partially from pure B2B infrastructure into a consumer product. ✅ ([Ramp Swaps Limited – Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/11850124)) ([Ramp Network rolls out multichain wallet — Cointelegraph](https://cointelegraph.com/news/ramp-network-self-custodial-wallet-third-party-dependencies))

**Critical disambiguation:** This entity is *not* the same as **ramp.com** ("Ramp Business Corporation"), the New York-based corporate-card / spend-management unicorn (founded 2019 by Eric Glyman, Karim Atiyeh, Gene Lee; valued at $32B in November 2025). The two companies have actually **litigated against each other at the USPTO TTAB** over the "Ramp" trademark (proceeding involving *Ramp Swaps LLC v. Ramp Business Corporation*, filed 23 June 2022; subsequently terminated). ✅ ([Ramp Swaps LLC v Ramp Business Corporation — Law360 TTAB](https://www.law360.com/ttab_cases/62b57eb1a1aba00a9ba671ca))

Self-claimed scale (mid-2025/early-2026 marketing): **8M+ customers**, 150+ countries, 100+ crypto assets, 40+ currencies, 160+ employees. 🟡 (marketing claim) ([Ramp Network About](https://rampnetwork.com/about))

## 8. Pivots and thesis evolution

**Original thesis (2017–2018):** Build a consumer Ethereum dApp. Failed because **fiat on-ramps were the binding constraint**. ✅

**Pivot 1 (2018):** Reframed as a **non-custodial fiat-to-crypto on-ramp**. Differentiator vs. Coinbase/Kraken: users buy crypto without ever depositing on an exchange — funds settle directly to the partner wallet. SDK/widget-first B2B distribution. ✅

**Expansion (2023):** **Off-ramp** added (FCA-driven), making them a two-way bridge. ✅

**Pivot 2 (2025–2026):** **Consumer self-custodial wallet** with built-in on/off-ramp, swaps, multichain (BTC, ETH, SOL, Arbitrum, Base, Optimism, plus 2 others), passkey auth. This is a meaningful strategic shift — competing directly with their own customers (MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Phantom). Wallet is *not* available in EU at launch (regulatory caution despite MiCAR). ✅ ([Ramp blog — multichain wallet](https://rampnetwork.com/blog/multichain-wallet-launch))

So the long arc is: **failed dApp → B2B on-ramp infra → two-way ramp → consumer wallet**.

## 10. Public-facing audits / compliance

- **SOC 2:** No public mention found of a Ramp Network SOC 2 attestation. The SOC 2/ISO/PCI material that surfaces in search results refers to the *other* Ramp (corporate cards). 🔴 (likely a real gap or simply not publicly disclosed)
- **ISO 27001:** No public Ramp Network certification found. 🔴
- **PCI DSS:** Required for card processing — Ramp processes cards via partners (Stripe, Visa rails, banking partners), so PCI scope is likely shared/inherited. No attestation publicly published. 🔴
- **Reserves attestation:** As a non-custodial flow-through (funds move user → Ramp → partner wallet), Ramp doesn't custody balances long-term, so reserve-attestation expectations are different from CEX/stablecoin issuers. No proof-of-reserves report exists or is expected. ✅
- **Regulator-mandated audits:** FCA AML reviews, CBI MiCAR ongoing supervision, AUP-style reporting under MTL regimes — these are confidential between Ramp and regulators.

This is a **gap relative to MoonPay** (which has published SOC 2) and a meaningful procurement-friction point for enterprise integrations.

The strategic question for AI × crypto / stablecoin payments work: **Ramp is structurally the most compliance-forward, EU-native onramp**, with first-cohort MiCAR authorisation but a much narrower US state footprint than US-native players. That asymmetry — strong EU/UK + thin US states + no APAC licensing — is the most useful framing for whether Ramp is the right partner for an AI-stablecoin product depending on your target geography.
