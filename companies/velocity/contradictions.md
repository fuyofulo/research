# Velocity - Contradictions and Open Tensions

Date: 2026-05-07

## 1. Founder/CEO narrative changed

May 2025 launch materials described Velocity as founded by Tom Greenwood and Eric Queathem, with Greenwood quoted as co-founder and CEO and Queathem as co-founder and President.

Current 2026 materials center Eric Queathem as Founder/CEO or President, and the current About page does not list Greenwood in the leadership team.

Companies House still lists Greenwood as an active PSC with more than 50% but less than 75% ownership/voting control.

Interpretation: operational leadership appears to have shifted toward Eric, but ownership/control filings still show Greenwood as important. Do not assume the reason without direct confirmation.

Sources: [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/05/28/3089291/0/en/Velocity-comes-out-of-stealth-with-10M-to-power-the-Velocity-of-Money.html), [About](https://www.velocity.xyz/about), [PSC page](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/16204089/persons-with-significant-control).

## 2. "Banking" language vs partner-dependent reality

Velocity markets a platform across banks, blockchains, and borders. It mentions regulated partners and compliant rails. But public pages do not clearly disclose which licenses Velocity itself holds.

Interpretation: likely a regulated-partner model with Velocity owning orchestration, product, compliance workflows, and customer experience. This is normal for fintech infrastructure, but the legal responsibilities matter.

## 3. "Global reach" vs hidden coverage

Homepage says global reach and 24/7/365 movement. Public docs do not disclose live countries, currencies, payment rails, or blockchains.

Interpretation: the network may be real but selectively available. Enterprise fintechs often hide coverage behind sales.

## 4. "One API" vs gated developer experience

Homepage and docs sell API integration. But the full docs are password-protected.

Interpretation: API probably exists for approved customers, but this is not self-serve infrastructure like Stripe/Bridge-style open docs.

## 5. "Real-time settlement" vs fiat edge constraints

Stablecoins settle 24/7. Fiat bank endpoints still depend on local banking rails, cutoff windows, holidays, risk review, and partner coverage.

Interpretation: Velocity can improve settlement but cannot magically make every fiat endpoint real-time.

## 6. AI positioning is weaker than stablecoin positioning

The original user suspicion included AI-native workflow competition. Current public Velocity positioning is mostly stablecoin, payments, settlement, treasury, compliance, and API. AI is not a deeply documented public product layer.

Interpretation: Velocity is a competitor to the stablecoin CFO stack, but not yet clear as an AI-native CFO automation competitor.

## 7. Product appears live, but traction is not public

Velocity says it is live and working with select enterprise partners/customers. No named customer case studies or volume metrics were found.

Interpretation: likely private beta or controlled enterprise rollout.
