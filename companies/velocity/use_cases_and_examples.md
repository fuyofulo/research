# Velocity - Use Cases and Examples

Date: 2026-05-07

## Who Velocity serves

Velocity names three customer groups:

- Payment companies.
- Global businesses.
- Financial institutions.

The actual users are CFOs, treasurers, finance operations teams, payment operations teams, and compliance/risk teams.

Source: [homepage](https://www.velocity.xyz/).

## Use case 1: Payment company settlement

Problem:

A payment company collects money from customers in one market and needs to settle merchants, partners, or subsidiaries in another market. Traditional settlement creates delays, prefunding requirements, FX spread, and reconciliation work.

Velocity flow:

1. Payment company onboards to Velocity.
2. It connects bank accounts, stablecoin wallets, and beneficiaries.
3. It receives or converts funds into stablecoins.
4. Velocity routes settlement through stablecoin and fiat partners.
5. Funds arrive faster than traditional correspondent banking.
6. Finance team gets reporting, controls, and audit trail.

Why they would buy:

- Faster merchant settlement.
- Less capital trapped in prefunding.
- Better visibility into balances and movement.
- Single API over multiple rails.

## Use case 2: Global business treasury

Problem:

A global company holds balances across countries, banks, currencies, and subsidiaries. Cash visibility is delayed, and treasury teams move money defensively because settlement is slow.

Velocity flow:

1. CFO/treasury team uses Velocity as a unified account layer.
2. They hold fiat and stablecoin balances.
3. They move capital 24/7 between regions.
4. They use quotes to decide when/where to convert.
5. They sync reports to a TMS/ERP.

Why they would buy:

- Better global cash visibility.
- Faster internal treasury movement.
- Reduced FX and correspondent banking friction.
- Programmable payment rules.

## Use case 3: Financial institution stablecoin infrastructure

Problem:

A financial institution wants stablecoin settlement but does not want to assemble custody, compliance, liquidity, blockchain, and banking integrations from scratch.

Velocity flow:

1. FI integrates Velocity API.
2. Velocity handles wallets/custody partner orchestration.
3. Velocity exposes settlement and transfer primitives.
4. FI serves end customers while Velocity abstracts stablecoin plumbing.

Why they would buy:

- Faster market entry.
- Lower infrastructure burden.
- Compliance-oriented architecture.
- Partner network already assembled.

## Use case 4: Supplier and contractor payouts

Problem:

A company pays vendors or contractors in multiple countries. Traditional bank payouts are slow, expensive, and opaque.

Velocity flow:

1. Add beneficiaries.
2. Quote payout route.
3. Convert from source balance into target currency or stablecoin.
4. Send to wallet or bank account.
5. Reconcile payout status.

Why they would buy:

- Lower payout friction.
- Weekend/holiday operation.
- Less manual ops.
- Better settlement status.

## Use case 5: Stablecoin yield on treasury balances

Problem:

Idle stablecoin balances earn nothing unless treasury teams manually route into yield products, which increases operational and risk burden.

Velocity flow:

1. Customer holds stablecoin balance.
2. Velocity offers third-party issuer/protocol rewards where available.
3. Customer sees yield/rewards inside treasury interface.

Important caveat:

Velocity disclaims that yield and rewards are generated by third-party issuers/protocol partners and are not offered, guaranteed, or underwritten by Velocity. Availability varies by jurisdiction.

Source: [homepage footer](https://www.velocity.xyz/).
