# Velocity - Explain Like I Am New On The Team

Date: 2026-05-07

## What is Velocity?

Velocity is trying to make business money move faster.

Today, a company that operates globally usually has money spread across banks, countries, currencies, payment processors, and treasury systems. Moving money between those places is slow. Banks close on weekends. Cross-border wires can take days. FX is expensive. Finance teams do a lot of manual reconciliation.

Velocity says: use stablecoins as the fast settlement rail, but wrap them in enterprise controls so CFOs can actually use them.

## Simple analogy

Think of Velocity like a control room for company money.

Inside the control room, a finance team can:

- See balances.
- Move money.
- Convert between fiat and stablecoins.
- Pay beneficiaries.
- Receive funds.
- Route settlement through faster rails.
- Keep audit/compliance controls.
- Connect to existing treasury or ERP systems.

Behind the scenes, Velocity connects banks, stablecoin networks, custody providers, liquidity providers, compliance tools, and APIs.

## Why stablecoins matter here

Stablecoins can move 24/7. Banks usually do not.

That means a company can potentially settle money at night, on weekends, across borders, and between currencies much faster than with old bank rails.

But enterprises cannot just use random wallets. They need:

- KYB.
- AML monitoring.
- Travel Rule compliance.
- Secure custody.
- Approval policies.
- Audit logs.
- Segregated funds.
- Reporting.
- Legal/regulatory comfort.

Velocity is trying to package stablecoins so enterprises can use them without feeling like they are using a crypto tool.

## Who buys it?

Likely buyers:

- CFOs.
- Treasurers.
- Payment operations teams.
- Payment companies.
- Financial institutions.
- Global businesses with cross-border payments.

## What makes Velocity serious?

- Founder/team comes from Worldpay, Volt, banking, product, compliance, and global payments.
- Raised $10M pre-seed.
- Dragonfly invested later.
- Fireblocks and Zodia are named infrastructure/custody partners.
- Website has API, portal, docs, and trust center.
- They are hiring compliance/financial crime people.

## What is still unclear?

- How many customers are live.
- What countries are live.
- What currencies are live.
- What blockchains are live.
- Whether Velocity itself has licenses or relies mostly on partners.
- How good the API is because docs are password-protected.
- Whether AI is a real product feature today.

## How should we think about them?

Velocity is a serious competitor in the stablecoin CFO stack category. But they are going after large enterprise infrastructure. That is expensive, slow, compliance-heavy, and relationship-driven.

Our opportunity is to start narrower: pick a painful finance workflow, use existing rails like Bridge/Squads/Privy, and build the AI-native operator layer that makes finance work easier.
