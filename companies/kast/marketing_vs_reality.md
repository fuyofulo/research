# KAST - Marketing vs Reality

Date: 2026-05-08

## Short verdict

KAST is real, fast-growing, and strategically important. But the marketing language "banking alternative" hides a lot of partner dependency and custody/legal risk. It is not a bank, user balances are not deposit-insured, normal account stablecoin transfers are treated as sales to KAST, and many "unlimited/global" claims are subject to compliance, partner, country, and product limits.

## What is definitely real

- KAST has a live app and support center.
- It has detailed fee disclosures.
- It supports stablecoin deposits/withdrawals across multiple networks.
- It offers Visa cards and memberships.
- It raised a $10M seed and $80M Series A.
- It has named investor backing from QED, Left Lane, Peak XV, HSG, DST Global Partners.
- It has a M0 partnership for KAST digital dollars on Solana.
- It has a business waitlist and clear business roadmap.

## What is not proven

- Exact active user count.
- Exact revenue.
- Exact transaction volume methodology.
- Partner bank/issuer/custodian legal structure.
- Whether KAST keeps one-to-one reserves for all USD ledger balances.
- How much customer money is protected if KAST or a partner fails.
- Actual card approval/decline rate.
- Actual local payout success rate.
- Whether "unlimited" survives real compliance/risk scenarios.

## The tell

The tell is the Terms. Marketing says "hold stablecoins" and "spend stablecoins." Terms say that when users transfer stablecoins/crypto to KAST, the transfer is a sale to KAST, and KAST records a USD reference balance. KAST may use, pool, convert, transfer, pledge, or rehypothecate assets at its discretion.

That does not mean the product is fake. It means the product is a custodial fintech ledger, not a self-custodial stablecoin account for ordinary spending balances.

## Honest one-liner

KAST is a custodial stablecoin neobank that gives global users a practical USD/card/payout experience by combining partner banking, Visa cards, stablecoin rails, swap/liquidity providers, and KAST's own ledger/rewards layer, while pushing toward a proprietary M0-powered stablecoin and business banking stack.
