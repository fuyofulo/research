# KAST - Deep Dive

Date: 2026-05-08

## Executive summary

KAST is building a global money app powered by stablecoins. It is a consumer and business fintech product, not a bank. The core promise is simple: users can hold a USD balance, receive ACH/Fedwire into a virtual USD account, deposit stablecoins or crypto, spend globally through Visa cards, withdraw stablecoins, send fiat payouts to local bank rails, and earn through vault products.

The company is moving fast. Its March 2026 Series A announcement says KAST serves more than 1M users and processes close to $5B in annualized transaction volume. It raised $80M from QED Investors and Left Lane Capital, with Peak XV, HSG, and DST Global Partners participating. Earlier it raised a $10M seed led by HSG and Peak XV.

The critical diligence point is custody and legal ownership. KAST's Terms say stablecoin or crypto transferred into the platform is treated as a sale to KAST, and KAST credits the user with a USD-denominated reference balance. That is very different from a pure self-custodial wallet. KAST Earn is different again: it says USDKY or vault shares are held in the user's own Privy wallet on Solana/Base.

## What KAST sells

KAST sells a bundle of stablecoin banking-like products:

- USD account: receive ACH/Fedwire into a virtual USD bank account.
- Stablecoin wallet: receive and send supported stablecoins across supported networks.
- Card: Visa Platinum/Infinite cards accepted globally.
- Local payouts: send funds to local bank rails in 15+ currencies and 200+ countries.
- KAST Tag: instant internal transfer to other KAST users.
- Earn: USD Prime Vault and Gauntlet Alpha Vault.
- Memberships: Standard, Premium, Private with annual fees, cashback, points, and card perks.
- Business: waitlisted product for global accounts, instant payouts, virtual/team cards, payroll, FX/stablecoin conversion, and integrations.

## Legal/regulatory posture

KAST is explicit that it is not a bank. The footer says KAST Tech as KAST is a financial technology company, not a bank; virtual accounts, payment cards, custody, and on/off-ramp services are provided by licensed partners; services vary by jurisdiction and regulatory approval.

Terms identify "KAST Tech, Reg No. 16223, incorporated in Anjouan, Comoros" as the company, while regulated services may be provided by partners. The risk disclosure says accounts are not covered by insurance against losses and users may not recover money/crypto if KAST fails, becomes bankrupt, or liquidates.

Sources: [About/footer](https://www.kast.xyz/about), [Terms](https://www.kast.xyz/legal/terms-and-conditions-of-service), [Risk Disclosure](https://www.kast.xyz/legal/kast-risk-disclosure).

## Product maturity

KAST is beyond a landing page:

- Mobile app exists.
- Help center is detailed.
- Fees are published.
- USD accounts are documented.
- Supported stablecoin/network table exists.
- Cards have visible pricing/rewards.
- Local payout fees/rails are documented.
- KAST Earn has product pages.
- Business is still waitlisted/rolling out.

The weak spots are transparency around partners, risk, and claims:

- Card issuer is not named clearly on primary pages.
- USD account partner is named as "Leads Bank (USA)" in support docs, but this needs confirmation.
- KAST says 170+ countries on site, 190+ countries in funding coverage, and help docs say exact eligibility is visible during sign-up.
- "Unlimited" limits are subject to partner/compliance discretion in Terms.
- User funds in normal spending account are not government-insured and may be owned by KAST after transfer.

## Strategic read

KAST is not primarily trying to be a crypto wallet. It is trying to be a global neobank for people who already understand why USD/stablecoins matter:

- Crypto workers.
- Freelancers.
- High-inflation country users.
- Internationally mobile founders/creators.
- Remote workers.
- Crypto-native businesses.
- Users underserved by local banking.

The wedge is card spend. The moat attempt is the full financial loop: receive USD, deposit stablecoins, spend through Visa, send payouts, earn yield, and eventually use KAST's own stablecoin substrate.

## Competitive relevance

KAST is important to our research because it shows what happens when stablecoin rails become a consumer app:

- It abstracts chains into a USD balance.
- It uses partner banks and PSPs at fiat edges.
- It uses cards to make stablecoins useful instantly.
- It adds rewards/memberships to fund acquisition and premium positioning.
- It expands from consumer into business payouts/payroll.

For our B2B AI CFO stack, KAST is a signal that stablecoin accounts/cards/payouts are becoming table stakes. The differentiated layer will be workflow automation, reconciliation, controls, accounting, and intelligence.
