# KAST - Explain Like I Am New On The Team

Date: 2026-05-08

## What does KAST do in one sentence?

KAST lets people use stablecoins like a global bank account: receive USD, deposit crypto/stablecoins, spend with a Visa card, send local payouts, and earn yield.

## What problem exists before KAST?

Many people earn globally but their bank is local.

Examples:

- A freelancer in Asia gets paid by U.S. clients.
- A crypto worker gets paid in USDC.
- Someone in Argentina wants to hold dollars.
- A founder travels across countries and wants one card that works.
- A small business pays contractors in multiple countries.

Traditional banks are slow, local, expensive for cross-border transfers, and often unfriendly to crypto. Crypto wallets are fast, but normal merchants do not accept wallet payments. KAST sits between these worlds.

## Who buys/uses it?

Consumers:

- Freelancers.
- Remote workers.
- Crypto users.
- Traders.
- International travelers.
- High-inflation-market users.
- Global earners.

Businesses:

- Agencies.
- Early-stage founders.
- Crypto-first companies.
- Global teams paying contractors/vendors.

## What exactly happens inside the product?

1. User signs up and completes KYC.
2. KAST creates a USD account/ledger balance.
3. User funds it with ACH/Fedwire, stablecoins, or crypto.
4. If crypto is not already a stablecoin, KAST converts it.
5. User sees a USD balance.
6. User can spend through a Visa card.
7. User can send stablecoins out.
8. User can send local fiat payouts.
9. User can put funds into Earn vaults.
10. Rewards/cashback/points are credited depending on membership.

## What data, money, or state moves?

Data:

- Identity/KYC.
- Country/residency.
- Wallet addresses.
- Bank account details.
- Card transaction events.
- Payout beneficiary details.
- Compliance/risk flags.

Money/state:

- Stablecoins enter on-chain.
- KAST records a USD balance.
- Card network authorizes spend.
- Local PSPs execute payouts.
- Earn vault positions live in Privy wallets on Solana/Base.

## Why is this hard?

KAST has to stitch together:

- Crypto deposits.
- Stablecoin withdrawals.
- USD bank accounts.
- Card issuing.
- Fraud checks.
- KYC/KYT/AML.
- Local payouts.
- FX.
- Rewards.
- Yield products.
- Country-by-country restrictions.

Each piece has a different partner, regulator, risk, and failure mode.

## Why now?

Stablecoins are mainstream enough that users want to spend them. Visa/card issuing infrastructure can connect crypto balances to merchants. Partner banks/PSPs make fiat edges possible. Emerging markets and remote work create real demand for USD access.

## What is actually impressive?

KAST makes stablecoins feel like a normal money app. The user does not need to think about bridges, DEXs, off-ramps, or bank wire mechanics. They just deposit, see USD, spend, send, or earn.

## What is still unclear?

- Whether user funds are fully protected.
- Which partners hold/issue what.
- Exact reserve structure.
- Actual active users and volume.
- Actual card/payout success rates.
- How KAST handles stress events.
- Whether Business becomes a real CFO product or stays a payout/card extension.

## Mental model

Think of KAST as "Revolut for stablecoin people": a polished app that hides crypto and banking complexity behind one USD balance, one card, and one payout interface.
