# Corgi - Marketing vs Reality

Date: 2026-05-08

## Short verdict

Corgi is real and unusually ambitious. The company has strong public proof: YC, named customers, $268M+ raised, $1.3B valuation, producer licenses in 45 jurisdictions, detailed disclaimers, regulatory approval, and a public ETF adviser side business. But the marketing phrase "full-stack carrier" compresses a lot of legal complexity. The reality is a vertically integrated group/program structure with producers, affiliated carriers, partner carriers, claims admins, risk retention structures, and reinsurance.

## What is definitely real

- Founded 2024, YC S24.
- Founders: Nico Laqua and Emily Yuan.
- Team size 70 on YC.
- $108M seed + Series A announced Jan 2026.
- $160M Series B at $1.3B valuation announced May 2026.
- Startup insurance products are live.
- Named customers and case studies exist.
- Producer license list exists across 45 jurisdictions.
- Corgi discloses AI/automated tools across core insurance operations.
- Corgi Strategies launched ETFs and appears in SEC filings.

## What is not proven

- Actual audited revenue.
- Loss ratio.
- Gross written premium.
- Net earned premium.
- Claim payout speed.
- Claim approval/denial rates.
- Automation rate.
- Model quality.
- Whether Corgi-controlled carriers underwrite the majority of policies.
- Reinsurance/risk retention economics.
- Whether "$40M ARR" maps to SaaS-like revenue or insurance premium/commission run-rate.

## The tell

Corgi's tell is the footer and disclaimers. The landing page says "we own our carrier" because that is the clean customer story. The legal docs reveal a more complex insurance architecture: risk retention group, affiliated carriers, partner carriers, producer entities, claims administrators, reinsurance, and jurisdiction-specific availability.

That is not necessarily bad. It is how insurance works. But it means the company should be evaluated like a regulated insurance platform, not like a pure AI SaaS app.

## Honest one-liner

Corgi is a fast-growing AI-native insurance program/carrier group that uses software and AI to sell startup business insurance faster than brokers, while building the regulated machinery needed to underwrite, issue, administer, and expand insurance and financial products.
