# Corgi (corgi.insure) - Research Folder

Compiled May 2026 using the company research playbook.

## Headline finding

Corgi is an AI-native, full-stack insurance and financial infrastructure company. The core product today is startup business insurance: founders can apply online, get an instant or near-instant quote, bind same-day, receive insurance documents/certificates, and manage coverage from a dashboard. The deeper bet is not just "better brokerage"; Corgi is trying to control underwriting, policy design, claims, distribution, and eventually adjacent financial products under one AI-first operating system.

The honest one-liner: Corgi is a YC S24 insurtech that turned startup insurance from a broker-mediated, multi-week workflow into a high-velocity founder checkout flow, backed by licensed producer entities, affiliated/partner risk-bearing carriers, claims operations, and AI systems across quoting, underwriting, pricing, policy issuance, fraud, claims, and service.

## Why it matters

Corgi is one of the cleanest examples of a modern AI company attacking a regulated back-office industry by owning the workflow end to end:

- Pick a niche with painful manual ops: startup insurance.
- Make the first wedge dead simple: quote and bind quickly.
- Use AI where workflows are document-heavy and rules-heavy.
- Do not stay at "software layer"; move into the regulated/risk-bearing stack.
- Use capital aggressively to expand product lines, distribution, and verticals.

This matters for us because the pattern is reusable: the company is not impressive because it says "AI"; it is impressive because it compresses a messy regulated workflow into a fast product experience.
