# Corgi - Explain Like I Am New On The Team

Date: 2026-05-08

## What does Corgi do in one sentence?

Corgi helps startups buy business insurance quickly, using AI and a full-stack insurance structure to handle quoting, underwriting, policy documents, claims, and coverage upgrades.

## What problem exists before Corgi?

Startup insurance is painful.

A founder usually only thinks about insurance when someone forces them:

- An enterprise customer asks for proof of insurance.
- An investor asks for D&O.
- A landlord asks for CGL.
- A SOC 2/security process needs insurance docs.
- The startup hires employees and needs more coverage.

Then the founder talks to a broker, fills forms, waits for quotes, gets confused by policy names, and loses days or weeks.

## Who buys it?

Startup founders, COOs, operators, finance leads, and chiefs of staff.

## Who uses it day to day?

Usually a founder or ops/finance person. Later, legal/finance/customer success may use the docs and certificates when customers ask for proof of insurance.

## What exactly happens step by step?

1. Founder goes to Corgi.
2. Founder chooses stage/package or starts application.
3. Corgi asks for company details: revenue, projected revenue, funding, documents, business type.
4. Corgi's system classifies the risk.
5. AI/rules/underwriting systems produce recommended coverage and price.
6. If simple, founder gets quote quickly.
7. Founder pays.
8. Policy/certificate documents are generated.
9. Founder can send proof of insurance to customer/investor/landlord.
10. If the company grows, founder upgrades coverage in the dashboard.
11. If something goes wrong, founder files a claim and Corgi/claims admins handle the process.

## What data moves through the system?

- Company identity.
- Revenue and projected revenue.
- Funding raised.
- Industry and business model.
- Customer contract insurance requirements.
- Prior claims.
- Employee/headcount data.
- Documents and financial statements.
- Payment data.
- Policy documents.
- Claims documents.

## What money moves?

- Customer pays premium.
- Producer/program administrator may earn fees/commissions.
- Carrier takes underwriting risk and receives premium.
- Reinsurance may absorb part of the risk.
- Claims payments are made by the issuing carrier under policy terms.

## Why is this hard?

Insurance is regulated state by state. Pricing risk is hard. Policies have legal terms and exclusions. Claims require licensed processes. AI cannot simply deny or approve material claims without oversight. A company must manage underwriting, compliance, licensing, fraud, claims, payments, and customer service.

## Why now?

AI startups and SaaS companies are selling into enterprises earlier. Enterprise buyers require insurance before signing. Founders expect Stripe-like onboarding, not broker email chains. AI can reduce the manual review burden across forms, documents, underwriting, claims, and service.

## What is actually impressive?

Corgi did not just build a prettier broker website. It is building the regulated operating system underneath: licenses, carrier/program structure, claims operations, AI underwriting/pricing, and customer workflow.

## What is still unclear?

- How automated underwriting really is.
- How much risk Corgi keeps.
- Loss ratio.
- Exact revenue quality.
- Which policies are underwritten by Corgi-affiliated carriers vs partners.
- Whether claims are as fast as quoting.
- Whether they can expand beyond startups without losing speed.

## Mental model

Think of Corgi as "Stripe Checkout for startup insurance, but with the ambition to become the carrier, policy admin, claims system, and financial product factory underneath."
