# Corgi - Use Cases and Examples

Date: 2026-05-08

## Core buyer moment

Corgi wins when a startup suddenly needs insurance to unlock something else:

- Close an enterprise contract.
- Pass a procurement/security review.
- Satisfy investor/board requirements.
- Hire employees.
- Lease office space.
- Complete SOC 2 or vendor compliance.
- Add coverage after raising a round.

The buyer is usually a founder, COO, finance/operator, or chief of staff. The user is the same person or someone on the ops/finance team.

## Example 1: Intryc

Customer: Intryc, AI Quality Assurance & CX Training.

Problem:

Intryc handled data for large customers such as Deel and Factorial and needed compliance-ready insurance without spending days/weeks navigating policies.

Corgi flow:

1. Founder submits application.
2. Documents come back quickly.
3. A Slack channel is created.
4. Corgi packages coverage around the startup's risk.
5. Intryc gets enterprise-ready documents and guidance.

Public result:

- "Zero to covered in 5 minutes."
- Faster document delivery.
- Direct Slack support with founding team.
- Goal: fast-track SOC 2 compliance and secure six-figure enterprise contracts.

Source: [Intryc case study](https://www.corgi.insure/customers/intryc).

## Pattern across all use cases

The pattern is very consistent:

1. Startup has urgency.
2. Insurance is blocking a bigger goal.
3. Traditional insurance feels slow and confusing.
4. Corgi turns it into a software checkout + support flow.
5. Customer gets document/certificate quickly.
6. Customer returns when coverage needs change.

## Why this is a strong wedge

Insurance is normally a low-frequency purchase. Corgi makes it a high-urgency workflow by targeting moments where insurance blocks revenue or financing. That is why the product can grow even if founders do not wake up wanting to buy insurance.
