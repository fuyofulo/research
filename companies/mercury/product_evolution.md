# Mercury (mercury.com) — Product Evolution, 2017 → 2026

A quarter-by-quarter chronology of Mercury Technologies Inc., the fintech that wraps banking services from partner banks (Choice Financial Group, Evolve Bank & Trust, Column N.A., Patriot Bank) into a software-first experience for startups, and which is now (as of Apr 2026) under OCC conditional approval to operate Mercury Bank, N.A. as a national bank.

**Disambiguation:** This is mercury.com, founded by Immad Akhund, Max Tagher, and Jason Zhang. Not Mercury Layer, Mercury Insurance, Mercury Systems (defense), Mercury Marine (outboard motors), Mercury General, Mercury Fund, or Mercury Computer Systems.

Confidence legend: ✅ = primary source / direct company / mainstream press confirmed. 🟡 = single decent source or fuzzy date. 🔴 = inferred or contested.

## 2019 — Public launch year

### Q2 2019
- **April / May 2019 — Public launch of business banking.** Mercury opens its doors to startups: free FDIC-insured checking + savings, no minimum balance, no monthly fees, debit cards, ACH, wires, multi-user permissions, programmatic API access, all in a single dashboard. ~1,500 signups in week one. ✅ https://venturebeat.com/business/mercury-opens-bank-for-startups-with-funding-from-andreessen-horowitz-partner
- **Positioning at launch:** "Banking built for startups." Mercury explicitly anti-positions vs SVB's relationship-banker model and vs legacy small-business UX (Chase, BofA). ✅ https://medium.com/@vcnewsfr/mercurys-thoughtful-composed-and-inevitable-ascent-to-the-top-of-startup-banking-a4dc73e41510

## 2020 — Pandemic windfall, free wires, Treasury beta

### Q1–Q2 2020
- COVID lockdown collapses the rate of new business formation in March/April but Mercury's API-first onboarding gives it tailwinds vs in-person bank branches. 🟡
- **"A summer of fresh features"** — free domestic wires, linked accounts, expanded onboarding for international founders. ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/free-wires

### Q3 2020
- Mercury aggressively builds the "Tea Room" tier (premium / high-volume accounts) which becomes the gating mechanism for premium services like free international wires. ✅ https://mercurydocumentation.com/banking-provider-international-wires-zero-fees

### Q4 2020
- **December 2020 — Free international USD wires for Tea Room customers** (previously $20/wire). New SWIFT partners shorten transfer times to 1–3 days from up to 9 days. ✅ https://mercurydocumentation.com/banking-provider-international-wires-zero-fees / https://mercury.com/blog/faster-better-international-wires
- **December 2020 — Mercury Treasury beta** announced. Targets startups with idle VC capital: sweeps excess cash into a Vanguard Treasury MMF and a Morgan Stanley Ultra-Short Income Portfolio (MULSX). Built by 6 engineers + 3 designers over ~9 months. 🟡 (Note: the user prompt cited "Mercury Treasury launch June 2021" — the BETA was Dec 2020; the GA / public push was mid-2021. Both timelines are accurate, applied to different milestones.) ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/introducing-mercury-treasury / https://finledger.com/articles/mercury-launches-new-cash-management-product-for-startups/

## 2022 — Credit card, venture debt, bill pay, Africa controversy

### Q1 2022
- **March 1, 2022 — African-startup account restrictions controversy.** Mercury freezes a "dozen to 30" accounts associated with African startups (notably Nigerian YC alumni) without clear notice, citing partner-bank concerns over unusual activity patterns. Significant negative coverage; Mercury issues clarifications. ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2022/03/01/mercury-restricted-a-number-of-accounts-linked-to-african-startups-and-didnt-exactly-say-why/
- **March 15, 2022 — Mercury Venture Debt launch.** Direct loans to VC-backed startups. Initial target: $200M out the door year one, up to $1B in year two. Loans typically 30–50% of last VC round. The product is a frontal attack on SVB's venture-debt franchise. ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2022/03/15/startup-banking-service-mercury-jumps-into-debt-lending-to-take-on-silicon-valley-bank/

### Q2 2022
- **Mercury Personal private beta** opens to a small group of founders / investors as a premium consumer account experiment. 🟡 (Public reporting points to a private beta running into 2023 before April 2024 GA.) ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/17/fintech-banking-startup-mercury-is-expanding-into-consumer-banking/

### Q3 2022
- **September 12, 2022 — Mercury IO charge card launches.** Corporate "credit" card (technically a charge card, paid off each month from the connected Mercury account) with **flat 1.5% cashback** on every purchase including international, no annual fee, no personal guarantee, no credit check, 0% APR, unlimited virtual cards with custom limits. ✅ https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20220912005228/en/Mercury-Introduces-IO-A-New-Corporate-Credit-Card-To-Help-Startups-Scale-Their-Business / https://mercury.com/blog/introducing-our-new-credit-card-io

This is the first wedge into Brex/Ramp-territory spend management. ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/inside-mercury/october-2022-product-updates

## 2023 — SVB year. The defining year for Mercury.

### Q1 2023 — The SVB collapse window

- **March 8–10, 2023 — Silicon Valley Bank fails.** A bank run drains SVB; the FDIC takes it into receivership on Friday March 10. Startup Twitter melts down.
- **March 10–17, 2023 — Mercury onboards the panic.** In the five days following SVB's failure, Mercury reports **>8,700 new customers** and **>$2B in new deposits**. Mercury's previously-built compliance ops absorb the load without major outages — a key competitive moment vs Brex (which also took inflows but with worse onboarding experiences during the panic). ✅ https://fortune.com/2023/05/11/mercury-2-billion-deposits/
- **March 13, 2023 — Mercury Vault launch (during the bank run).** Expands FDIC insurance via sweep network to up to **$3M initially, then $5M** by sweeping across ~20 partner banks; idle cash optionally placed in a Treasury-focused MMF. This is a direct, real-time response to the SVB-uninsured-deposit panic. ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2023/03/13/mercury-expands-fdic-insurance-up-to-3m-through-new-vault-product/ / https://mercury.com/blog/mercury-vault-announcement / https://fintechlabs.com/reacting-quickly-to-svb-fallout-mercury-launches-vault-with-5m-fdic-deposit-insurance/

### Q2 2023
- **April 2023 — Vault scales to $5M FDIC, automated payroll, weekly card limits.** Product velocity peaks; Mercury ships nearly weekly. ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/april-2023-product-updates
- **April–June 2023 — ~17,000 additional new customers join.** ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2023/07/07/mercury-says-it-gained-nearly-26k-new-customers-in-the-four-months-after-svbs-collapse/

### Q3 2023
- **July 7, 2023 — 26K+ new customers in the four months since SVB.** Net retention is the eyebrow-raiser: **92% of those net-new March customers were still active six months later**, and deposits held steady — meaning the panic-onboarded cohort actually stayed. ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2023/07/07/mercury-says-it-gained-nearly-26k-new-customers-in-the-four-months-after-svbs-collapse/
- **Working Capital Loans for Ecommerce** — Mercury expands lending beyond venture debt with a flat-fee, fixed-weekly-repayment product for ecommerce brands needing inventory/marketing capital. 🟡 https://mercury.com/working-capital-loans

### Q4 2023
- **November 2023 — Invoice forwarding & merchant-blocking.** Auto-forward unpaid bills directly into the Payments queue; new card controls for blocked merchants. ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/november-2023-product-updates
- **"Ambitious companies" rebrand** crystallizes during 2023. Mercury drops the narrow "for startups" framing and recasts itself as banking + financial OS for "ambitious companies" — small businesses, ecommerce, professional services, scaled growth-stage firms. Justification: by Q3/Q4 2023 a meaningful share of new customers are not VC-backed startups. ✅ (Messaging visible across mercury.com homepage and 2024+ press; explicit framing in 2024 letter.) https://research.contrary.com/company/mercury
- **2023 transaction volume ≈ $95B** (vs $50B in 2022, ~90% YoY). ✅ https://x.com/immad/status/1892610068966989915

## 2024 — Synapse year. Migration, Personal GA, Column.

### Q1 2024
- **January 2024 — Synapse countersuit / public dispute.** Synapse files countersuit against Mercury, accusing Mercury of having taken ~$50M in end-user funds during migration (~$32M at migration + ~$17.7M post-migration debits). Mercury denies, calling the claims "knowingly meritless." ✅ https://fortune.com/2025/03/07/synapse-evolve-mercury-bankruptcy-lawsuits/
- **Federal Reserve cease-and-desist against Evolve Bancorp** (parent of Evolve Bank & Trust) cites BSA/AML, risk management, consumer protection failings in its fintech program — affects Mercury and other Evolve customers. ✅ https://www.americanbanker.com/news/fed-hits-synapse-partner-evolve-bank-with-cease-and-desist-order

### Q2 2024
- **April 17, 2024 — Mercury Personal launches (GA).** Premium consumer banking targeted at "builders" — founders, operators, investors. **$240/year subscription.** Features: high-yield savings (5% APY at launch), no-fee domestic wires + ACH, customizable debit-card limits, worldwide ATM fee reimbursement, $5M FDIC via sweep, multi-user permissions, auto-transfer rules. ✅ https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240417396140/en/Mercury-Launches-Personal-Banking / https://mercury.com/blog/inside-mercury/mercury-personal-launch
- **May 7, 2024 — Bill Pay + Spend Management push.** Mercury formally enters Brex/Ramp territory with full bill pay, expense management, accounting automations, multi-user spend policies. This is the "financial OS" thesis crystallizing in product. ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2024/05/07/startup-neobank-mercury-is-taking-on-brex-and-ramp-with-new-bill-pay-spend-management-software/
- **May 8, 2024 — Synapse collapse.** TabaPay's deal to acquire Synapse falls apart when Evolve refuses to cover an end-user fund shortfall. Synapse files Chapter 11. 100,000+ fintech consumers across many platforms lose access to ~$265M. Mercury — the largest Synapse customer at ~35% of Synapse's business — had **already** migrated ~$3B in deposits directly to Evolve months earlier, sparing most of its customers from the worst freezes (though the migration itself is the genesis of the $50M dispute). ✅ https://prospect.org/2024/05/23/2024-05-23-fintech-fight-frozen-bank-accounts-synapse/ / https://fortune.com/2025/03/07/synapse-evolve-mercury-bankruptcy-lawsuits/

### Q3 2024
- **July 23, 2024 — Geographic restrictions tighten.** Mercury announces it will close accounts of fintech founders based in Nigeria, Ukraine, and ~12 other countries (Zimbabwe, Mali, Pakistan, Philippines, Croatia, Venezuela, others). Stated reason: partner-bank compliance posture post-Evolve cease-and-desist. Significant blowback from African and Eastern European founder communities. ✅ https://techcrunch.com/2024/07/23/mercury-bank-fintech-sanctions-ukraine-nigeria/ / https://techpoint.africa/2024/07/23/mercury-bank-closes-african-accounrs/
- **August 2024 — Invoicing GA.** Branded invoicing (logo + colors), pay by card / Apple Pay / Google Pay / wire / ACH, auto-match payments to invoices, recurring invoices. Stripe powers the card-rails piece. ✅ https://stripe.com/customers/mercury / https://mercury.com/blog/september-2024-product-updates
- **September 2024 — Working capital for ecommerce expands.** ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/september-2024-product-updates

### Q4 2024
- **October 2024 — Column N.A. added as direct partner bank.** Strategic move: Column is itself a developer-focused, FDIC-insured nationally chartered bank (William Hockey / Hadley Harris). For Mercury, it adds same-day ACH, non-USD wire acceptance, and most importantly, **resiliency**: a third major partner bank reduces single-bank concentration after the Synapse/Evolve trauma. ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/mercury-column-na / https://x.com/mercury/status/1847993681006985514
- **October 2024 product updates** — broader workflow enhancements. ✅ https://mercury.com/blog/inside-mercury/october-2024-product-updates
- **2024 full-year metrics (reported Feb 2025):**
  - **Revenue: $500M** (from ~$300M-range in 2023) ✅ https://sacra.com/research/mercury-at-500m-annualized-revenue/
  - **Transaction volume: $156B** (up 64% YoY from ~$95B) ✅ https://x.com/immad/status/1892610068966989915
  - **Customers: ~200K+** (~40% YoY growth) ✅ https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250326427283/

## Founder narrative — Immad Akhund's strategic statements over time

- **2017–2019 — "Bank for startups."** The original frame: build the bank account YC founders actually want — API-first, no minimums, no monthly fees, fast onboarding, multi-user permissions out of the box.
- **Post-SVB (mid–late 2023) — "We are the contingency."** Akhund publicly positions Mercury as the resilient counterweight to a one-bank-relationship model. The Vault FDIC sweep and aggressive coverage of $3M → $5M is the productized version of this argument.
- **Post-Synapse (late 2024) — "The hundred-program BaaS model is broken."** In multiple interviews (Fintech Blueprint, Bank On It, podcasts) Akhund argues that Synapse-style BaaS aggregator middleware cannot survive the reconciliation and consumer-protection burden; Mercury's response is the multi-direct-bank model and ultimately its own charter. ✅ https://podcasts.apple.com/ng/podcast/inside-mercurys-650m-revenue-machine-with-ceo-immad/id1549588039
- **2024 → 2025 — "Ambitious companies, not just startups."** Public rebrand and customer-mix reflect this. By 2025 letter: 73% of new customers from outside tech/AI startup category. ✅ https://x.com/immad/status/2019447745480913256

## Strategic arc — one-paragraph summary

Mercury launched in 2019 as a thin, beautiful software wrapper on top of someone else's bank charter, betting that startups would defect from legacy banks if onboarding and dashboards became 10× better. Two macro shocks — the SVB collapse of March 2023 and the Synapse collapse of May 2024 — converted Mercury from a niche YC-founder bank account into the default operating system for SMB and growth-stage finance. SVB taught Mercury the value of being the contingency (Vault, sweep network, $5M FDIC) and gave it a free customer-acquisition flywheel; Synapse taught it the brittleness of BaaS middleware and pushed it toward direct multi-bank relationships (Column N.A. added Oct 2024) and eventually its own OCC national bank charter (filed Dec 2025, conditional approval Apr 2026). In parallel, Mercury made the classic horizontal-expansion sweep — credit card (IO, 2022), bill pay (2022), venture debt (2022), invoicing (2024), spend management (2024), AI-native search and MCP agent rails (2025), and now native payroll via the Central acquisition (April 2026) — turning a checking account into a financial operating system. Revenue went from indeterminate (2019) → ~$300M (2023) → $500M (2024) → $650M+ annualized (Sep 2025), with four consecutive years of GAAP profitability — a remarkably rare profile for a $3.5B–$5B-valued fintech in this era. The single biggest strategic pivot was the late-2023 / 2024 rebrand from "banking for startups" to "for ambitious companies," because it unlocked an addressable market 20× larger than the startup ecosystem and is the implicit thesis behind every subsequent product and corporate move: the charter, the payroll acquisition, the consumer-banking expansion, and the AI-agent rails.
