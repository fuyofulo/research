## Disambiguation

Throughout this dossier, **"Mercury"** refers to **Mercury Technologies, Inc.**, the San Francisco-headquartered fintech (mercury.com) co-founded by Immad Akhund, Max Tagher, and Jason Zhang in 2017. It is a **non-bank** fintech that offers banking products via partner banks (Choice Financial Group, Column N.A., Patriot Bank N.A.), with a conditionally-approved national bank charter (April 2026) under the name **Mercury Bank, N.A.**

It is **NOT**:
- **Mercury Layer / Mercury Wallet** — Bitcoin / statechain product.
- **Mercury Insurance** — Mercury General Corporation (NYSE: MCY), auto insurer.
- **Mercury Systems, Inc.** — NASDAQ: MRCY, defense electronics.
- **Mercury Marine** — Brunswick subsidiary, outboard engines.
- **Mercury Retirement / Mercury Financial** — unrelated consumer credit card issuer.
- **Mercury Public Affairs / Mercury LLC** — DC lobbying firm previously tied to Russian sanctions controversy ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/u-s-firm-mercury-llc-tried-to-enlist-ambassadors-to-help-russian-company/)).

Every claim below has been cross-checked to apply specifically to **mercury.com / Mercury Technologies, Inc.**

### Mercury Vault sweep network (verified)
- Mercury Vault (launched March 2023 after the SVB collapse) provides up to **\$5M in FDIC insurance** by spreading deposits across **up to ~20 program banks** in the partner bank's sweep network ([Mercury blog: Vault launch](https://mercury.com/blog/mercury-vault-announcement); [Mercury support: FDIC insurance](https://support.mercury.com/hc/en-us/articles/28776140568212-Understanding-FDIC-insurance); [Fintech Labs](https://fintechlabs.com/reacting-quickly-to-svb-fallout-mercury-launches-vault-with-5m-fdic-deposit-insurance/)).

- Direct strategic response to the **Silicon Valley Bank failure** of March 2023.

## 5. Synapse Fallout (May 2024)

### Mercury's role pre-collapse (verified)
- Mercury had originally used **Synapse Financial Technologies** as its banking-as-a-service middleware on top of **Evolve Bank & Trust**.
- At peak, Mercury **accounted for ~35% of Synapse's business** ([Fortune, Mar 7 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/03/07/synapse-evolve-mercury-bankruptcy-lawsuits/)).

### Migration off Synapse — late 2023 (verified)
- Mercury moved to a **direct relationship with Evolve** between **September 28 – October 10, 2023**, eliminating Synapse as middleware ([Sankaet Pathak on Medium](https://medium.com/@sankaet/mercury-reconciliation-issues-7f3c8dec9b93)).
- Mercury moved **>\$3 billion in deposits** out of the Synapse-routed FBO accounts.

### The \$49.6M reconciliation dispute (verified)
- Synapse founder **Sankaet Pathak publicly alleged** Mercury took **\$49,623,829.47 more** than it should have during the migration — implying Evolve mistakenly over-disbursed.
- Mercury **flatly denied** this, saying it followed Evolve's migration process and all customer funds were accounted for ([Banking Dive on TabaPay collapse](https://www.bankingdive.com/news/tabapay-calls-off-its-planned-acquisition-of-synapse-mercury-evolve-bank-and-trust/715750/); [Sankaet Medium](https://medium.com/@sankaet/mercury-reconciliation-issues-7f3c8dec9b93)).
- Mercury **filed \$30M arbitration** against Synapse alleging Synapse withheld Mercury revenue ([FinTech Business Weekly](https://fintechbusinessweekly.substack.com/p/mercury-seeking-30m-from-synapse)).

### The May 2024 collapse (verified)
- **May 8, 2024**: TabaPay pulled out of acquiring Synapse, partly because of the unresolved Mercury/Evolve/Synapse reconciliation dispute ([TechCrunch, May 9 2024](https://techcrunch.com/2024/05/09/tabapay-ends-plans-synapse-mercury-evolve/)).
- **May 11, 2024**: Evolve lost access to Synapse's dashboard. Customer funds at **Yotta, Juno, Copper, and others** (NOT Mercury — Mercury had already exited) were frozen ([American Prospect, May 23 2024](https://prospect.org/2024/05/23/2024-05-23-fintech-fight-frozen-bank-accounts-synapse/)).
- ~\$200M in end-user funds were ultimately frozen / unaccounted ([Fortune, Mar 7 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/03/07/synapse-evolve-mercury-bankruptcy-lawsuits/)).

### Mercury's exposure to the public crisis (verified)
- Mercury was **not** part of the frozen-account user pool (its customers were on Evolve direct, not Synapse, by May 2024).
- But Mercury was a **major narrative figure**: Synapse blamed Mercury for the \$49.6M shortfall; Yotta-class plaintiffs cited Mercury in lawsuits.
- Mercury issued statements through 2024-2025 emphasizing its customers were unaffected and pushed forward with the Choice migration.

### "NYT investigation" — clarification (verified)
The prompt cited a **NYT investigation**; the **actual primary investigation was by The Information**, not the New York Times. Specifically:
- **March 28, 2024 — The Information, Michael Roddan**: *"Inside Mercury's Stumble From Fintech Hero to Target of the Feds"* ([The Information article](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/inside-mercurys-stumble-from-fintech-hero-to-target-of-the-feds); [Michael Roddan tweet](https://twitter.com/MichaelRoddan/status/1773336692193108415); [Threads / The Information](https://www.threads.net/@theinformation/post/C5Dx6RKuiy9/read-our-latest-investigation)).
- Findings:
  - Mercury opened **thousands of accounts via Choice Bank for foreign companies** using shaky US-presence proofs (PO boxes from registered-agent services).
  - Mercury **paid affiliate commissions to LLC University and Firstbase.io** to send foreign customers into the Mercury / Choice funnel.
  - Mercury allegedly **did not file a SAR** on an attempted ATM transaction from a client in **Cuba** — a comprehensively sanctioned jurisdiction.
  - Mercury's compliance system was flagging **a suspiciously low rate of suspicious transactions**, which FDIC examiners said Choice had not vetted.
  - FDIC officials told Choice this in a **July 2023 meeting** following a joint FDIC + ND DFI exam.
- This is summarized in [Fintech Business Weekly: Mercury's High Risk, High Rewards Strategy](https://fintechbusinessweekly.substack.com/p/mercurys-high-risk-high-rewards-strategy) and [Fintech Takes: I Don't Understand Mercury](https://fintechtakes.com/articles/2024-07-22/i-dont-understand-mercury/).

### Geography purge — July 2024 (verified)
- **July 23, 2024**: Mercury abruptly told customers it was **closing accounts in 13 African countries plus Ukraine, Croatia, Philippines, Pakistan, Venezuela** and other jurisdictions, effective **August 22, 2024** ([TechCrunch, Jul 23 2024](https://techcrunch.com/2024/07/23/mercury-bank-fintech-sanctions-ukraine-nigeria/); [TechCabal](https://techcabal.com/2024/07/23/us-bank-mercury-to-close-accounts-of-nigerian-startups/); [Norebase blog](https://blog.norebase.com/compliance-update-mercury-bank-set-to-shut-down-accounts-in-13-african-countries/)).
- Stated reason: *"recent changes in how we determine account eligibility."*
- Real reason (widely interpreted): consequence of the Choice consent order's CIP/CDD lookback requirements and FATF greylist exposure.

## 6. FDIC Consent Order on Choice Financial Group

### Key facts (verified)
- **Choice Financial Group** (Fargo, ND) entered into a **consent order with the FDIC and the North Dakota Department of Financial Institutions** issued on **January 26, 2024** ([FDIC orders search, January 2024](https://orders.fdic.gov/s/press-release-orders?prYear=2024&prDate=26&prMonth=1); [The Bank Slate, Jan 2024](https://www.thebankslate.com/2024/01/choice-in-nd-hit-with-consent-order-tied-to-third-parties/)).
- Order stems from a **2023 joint exam** that uncovered Bank Secrecy Act / AML violations specifically tied to Choice's **fintech-partner programs** (Mercury, Current, Lili) ([Jason Mikula on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasonmikula_breaking-part-two-choice-bank-partner-activity-7156671961404743680-7xwW)).

### What the order required Choice to do (verified)
1. Improve **board oversight** of the BSA/AML program, specifically as it pertains to third-party fintech partners.
2. Establish a dedicated **AML/CFT compliance committee**.
3. Revise and strengthen the bank's **AML/CFT program**.
4. Tighten **internal controls**.
5. Implement an **appropriate Customer Identification Program (CIP)** — i.e., real verified addresses, not PO boxes.
6. Implement an **appropriate Customer Due Diligence program (CDD)** even when end-customers are onboarded via fintech partners.
7. Implement proper **suspicious activity monitoring and reporting**.
8. Conduct a **lookback review** of customers/transactions onboarded via third parties flagged in the bank's 2023 internal report. Choice was ordered to do a **4-year CIP lookback**.
9. Add appropriately resourced and independent compliance staffing.

### Cause (verified)
The cited triggers (most also surfaced in The Information's Roddan reporting):
- Mercury accounts opened in **legally risky countries** that Choice's onboarding had let through.
- **Thousands of foreign-entity accounts** opened via Mercury using "questionable" US-presence proofs.
- A compliance / transaction-monitoring system **Mercury was using that Choice had not independently vetted** and that flagged "curiously low" suspicious-activity volumes.

### Mercury's exposure (verified)
- Mercury was the **named driver of the volume** in the third-party program.
- Mercury immediately tightened eligibility (the July 2024 country purge) and beefed up compliance — risk and compliance now reportedly ~20% of headcount ([Sacra: Mercury at \$500M ARR](https://sacra.com/research/mercury-at-500m-annualized-revenue/); [Fortune, Nov 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/11/07/exclusive-mercury-fintech-valuation-650-million-2025-annualized-revenue-immad-akhund-interview/)).
- Public commentary on Mercury's "high-risk strategy" colliding with regulatory reality: [Fintech Business Weekly](https://fintechbusinessweekly.substack.com/p/mercurys-high-risk-high-rewards-strategy).

### Status of the lookback (single-source)
- The order required time-bound compliance; subsequent FDIC enforcement actions list has no public termination notice as of late 2025. Treat ongoing compliance as in-progress.

## 7. Regulatory Posture

### Mercury is NOT a bank (as of 2025-2026) (verified)
- Mercury operates as a fintech that distributes regulated banking products through partner banks.
- All deposit accounts are technically held at **Choice Financial Group, Column N.A., or Patriot Bank N.A.** (Members FDIC).

### National bank charter pursuit (verified)
- **December 19, 2025**: Mercury **filed applications with the OCC** (national bank charter) and **FDIC** (deposit insurance) to charter **Mercury Bank, N.A.** ([Mercury blog, Dec 19 2025](https://mercury.com/blog/occ-national-bank-charter-application); [Bloomberg, Dec 19 2025](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-19/fintech-firm-mercury-applies-for-national-bank-charter); [Banking Dive, Dec 19 2025](https://www.bankingdive.com/news/fintech-mercury-apply-occ-national-bank-charter-fdic-fed-jon-auxier-sofi/808411/); [Business Wire, Dec 19 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20251219760269/en/Mercury-Applies-for-OCC-National-Bank-Charter-to-Become-the-Bank-for-Builders)).
- **April 27, 2026**: **OCC granted conditional approval** ([Business Wire, Apr 27 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260427949683/en/Mercury-Receives-OCC-Conditional-Approval-to-Establish-Mercury-Bank-N.A.); [Banking Dive, Apr 27 2026](https://www.bankingdive.com/news/mercury-nabs-conditional-occ-charter/818674/); [Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2026/04/276575-mercury-bank-approved-for-national-bank-charter/)). Conditional, so Mercury still needs:
  - To meet remaining OCC conditions.
  - FDIC deposit-insurance approval.
  - Federal Reserve approval.
- ~4 months from application → conditional approval, which is fast and notable.
- **Akhund's framing** (Mercury blog, Dec 2025): *"Our customers have been asking for Zelle, for expanded lending, for payment infrastructure we actually control. We couldn't give them those things without a bank charter."*

### California DFPI settlement — 2021/2022 (verified)
- The California **Department of Financial Protection and Innovation** found Mercury used the **FDIC logo** in marketing in violation of **Cal. Fin. Code §§ 561 and 562** (laws prohibiting unlicensed entities from holding out as banks).
- A settlement was reached and dated **January 2022** ([DFPI Settlement Agreement, Jan 2022 PDF](https://dfpi.ca.gov/wp-content/uploads/sites/337/2022/01/Admin.-Action-Mercury-Technologies-Inc.-Settlement-Agreement.pdf); [DFPI enforcement action page](https://dfpi.ca.gov/enforcement_action/mercury-technologies-inc/)).
- Same enforcement template DFPI applied to **Chime** in 2021.

### FDIC comment letter — October 2024 (verified)
- Mercury submitted a **comment letter** to the FDIC's **request for information on bank-fintech arrangements** dated **October 30, 2024**, signed by Immad Akhund ([FDIC Federal Register: RIN-ZA43](https://www.fdic.gov/federal-register-publications/mercury-technologies-immad-akhund-rin-za43); [Federal Reserve comments docket](https://www.federalreserve.gov/apps/proposals/comments/FR-0000-0134-01-C11)).

## Key narrative arc (synthesis)

1. **2017-2022**: Build a beautiful UX for startup banking on top of partner banks; survive on interchange + small interest share; nobody really notices.
2. **March 2023 SVB collapse**: Inflection point. Mercury absorbs >\$2B in deposits in days, doubles customer count.
3. **Late 2023**: Mercury exits Synapse middleware (smart move in retrospect), goes direct-to-Evolve.
4. **Jan 2024**: Choice Bank consent order; the BSA/AML weaknesses partly traced back to Mercury's onboarding.
5. **March 2024**: The Information's Roddan investigation goes public — affiliate-commissioned foreign LLCs, PO-box addresses, missing SARs on Cuba transactions.
6. **April 2024**: Mercury launches consumer banking (Mercury Personal).
7. **May 2024**: Synapse implodes around (but not on top of) Mercury.
8. **July 2024**: Mercury purges 13+ countries from its customer base.
9. **March 2025**: \$300M Series C at \$3.5B from Sequoia (first check) + Evolve exit announcement.
10. **Nov 2025**: Fortune profile — \$650M ARR, profitable, 300K customers.
11. **Dec 2025**: Applies for national bank charter. Hires SoFi's Auxier to run it.
12. **Apr 2026**: OCC conditional approval — 4 months from filing.

The bet from here: Mercury becomes a **chartered national bank with a SaaS-grade product surface**, distinct from both legacy banks (poor UX) and BaaS-dependent fintechs (regulatory fragility).
