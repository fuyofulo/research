# Flex - Explain Like I Am New On The Team

Date: 2026-05-09

## What does Flex do in one sentence?

Flex gives business owners one platform for banking, credit, bill pay, expense cards, global payments, and AI-powered finance workflows.

## What problem exists before Flex?

Business owners use too many financial tools:

- One bank for deposits.
- One card for spend.
- One AP tool for bills.
- One expense tool for employee cards.
- One wire/FX provider for international vendors.
- One accounting system.
- A private bank or personal finance team separately.

That creates fragmented visibility. The owner cannot easily answer: how much cash do I have, what bills are due, what vendors need payment, how much credit float do I have, which employees are spending, and what can I safely invest back into growth?

## Who buys it?

Middle-market business owners and operators. Flex is especially interested in high-net-worth owner-operators with businesses large enough to need meaningful credit limits, payment workflows, and support.

## Who uses it day to day?

- Owner/founder/CEO.
- Finance lead.
- Controller/bookkeeper.
- Employees using cards.
- Admins approving payments.

## What exactly happens step by step?

1. Business applies to Flex.
2. Flex/KYB/underwriting reviews the business.
3. If approved, business opens banking and/or credit products.
4. Owner funds bank account by ACH/wire.
5. Owner issues employee cards and sets limits.
6. Business uploads invoices or forwards bills.
7. Flex AI extracts bill data.
8. Admin approves payments.
9. Payments go out by ACH, wire, card, or global payment rail.
10. Transactions sync to accounting systems.
11. Flex uses data to increase credit/offer more products/produce insights.

## What data moves through the system?

- Business identity.
- Owner identity.
- Bank transactions.
- Card transactions.
- Vendor details.
- Invoice PDFs/emails.
- Approval events.
- Receipts/memos.
- Accounting categories.
- Payment status.
- Credit risk signals.

## What money moves?

- Deposits through Thread Bank.
- Credit spend through Lead Bank-issued Flex card.
- ACH/wires through bank rails.
- International payments through Visa/Currencycloud rails.
- Card payments through Visa.
- Bill Pay payments through ACH/wire/card.

## Why is this hard?

Flex sits across banking, lending, cards, AP, FX, compliance, fraud, accounting integrations, and customer support. Each part has different partners and risks. Credit is especially hard because Flex must decide how much float to offer and manage repayment/default risk.

## Why now?

Business owners expect software-grade finance. Sponsor banks, card issuing, embedded payments, accounting APIs, and AI document/workflow tools make it possible to bundle services that used to require banks, cards, AP tools, and private bankers separately.

## What is actually impressive?

The product wedge is concrete: Net-60 credit and better cash flow. From there, Flex expands into the rest of the finance stack. It is not trying to sell "AI" alone; it uses AI to improve high-friction financial workflows.

## What is still unclear?

- How much of AI is live vs roadmap.
- Credit loss rates.
- Revenue quality.
- Customer count.
- Whether personal/private banking is meaningfully launched.
- Whether global payments quality works equally across all marketed countries.

## Mental model

Think of Flex as "Brex/Ramp + business banking + Bill.com + private-bank concierge for owner-operators, with AI agents becoming the glue."
