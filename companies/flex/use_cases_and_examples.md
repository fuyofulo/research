# Flex - Use Cases and Examples

Date: 2026-05-09

## Core buyer moment

Flex wins when the owner has a profitable business but messy cash flow:

- Vendor bills due before customer revenue arrives.
- Need bigger credit limits than traditional cards provide.
- Multiple entities/accounts/cards are hard to manage.
- Owner wants premium service without private-bank slowness.
- International vendors are annoying to pay.
- AP and expense tracking create manual work.

## Example 1: VIP Completions

Customer:

VIP Completions, private aviation / aircraft interior design, South Florida, 50-100 employees.

Problem:

High upfront costs for custom interior projects. Supplier/material costs came due before client deposits or project revenue fully aligned. The company risked cash-flow constraints and personal capital exposure under short 30-day payment terms.

Flex flow:

1. VIP Completions uses Flex Business Credit Card.
2. Flex provides 60-day float.
3. VIP pre-purchases premium materials without draining cash reserves.
4. Project spend aligns better with customer revenue timing.
5. Business can take on larger/high-value projects.

Public result:

- Eliminated cash-flow constraints caused by short 30-day payment terms.
- Used 60-day float to gain financial runway.

Source: [customers page](https://www.flex.one/customers).

## Example 2: International vendor payments

Customer:

Kash Dhuna / Dhuna Ventures testimonial.

Problem:

Sending foreign wires through Fidelity was painful.

Flex flow:

1. User sets up international recipient.
2. Selects USD or one of supported foreign currencies.
3. Chooses Flex account or Flex credit card.
4. Recipient receives payment directly to bank account.

Public result:

Customer said sending foreign wires with Flex felt "light years ahead" of Fidelity.

Sources: [global payments](https://www.flex.one/global-payments), [customers](https://www.flex.one/customers).

Customer:

## Example 5: Bill Pay / AP automation

User:

Any business owner/operator receiving vendor invoices by PDF/email.

Flow:

1. Upload invoice or forward by email.
2. Flex AI extracts invoice data.
3. Payment enters visual approval workflow.
4. Admin approves.
5. Payment goes out by ACH, same-day ACH, wire, or card.
6. Payment syncs to accounting software.

Why Flex wins:

- Less manual entry.
- Central approvals.
- Same-day ACH and wires.
- Option to use credit/float for bills.

Source: [bill pay](https://www.flex.one/bill-pay).

## Example 6: Global owner/operator with U.S. company

User:

Global entrepreneur with U.S. company/current or planned U.S. operations.

Flow:

1. Applies to Flex.
2. Opens business banking if approved.
3. Receives ACH/wire account details.
4. Issues debit/credit cards.
5. Pays vendors globally.
6. Uses AP/expense/accounting sync.

Why Flex wins:

Flex says businesses do not need the owner physically in the U.S.; they need current/planned U.S. operations and a U.S. or international residential address, not registered agent/PO box/UPS box.

Source: [business banking FAQ](https://www.flex.one/business-banking).
