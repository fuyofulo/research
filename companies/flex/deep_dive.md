# Flex - Deep Dive

Date: 2026-05-09

## Executive summary

Flex is a fintech platform for middle-market business owners. Its current marketing says "AI-Native Finance Platform for Business Owners" and "Designed for the 1%, now available to entrepreneurs globally." The product suite includes business banking, a Net-60 credit card, employee expense management, global payments, bill pay/AP automation, accounts receivable, pay-by-card, and an emerging agentic ERP/AI CFO layer.

Flex is not a bank. Banking services are provided by Thread Bank, Member FDIC. The debit card is issued by Thread Bank. The Flex Business Credit Card is issued by Lead Bank. International payment services are tied to Visa Global Services Inc. / Currencycloud terms. This is a modern sponsor-bank/card/FX partner stack wrapped in a premium owner-facing UX.

## What Flex sells

Current product categories:

- Business banking: checking, ACH/wires, debit cards, subaccounts, APY, FDIC sweep coverage up to $3M via Thread Bank/program banks.
- Business credit card: 3-in-1 card, Net-60 terms, cashback/points, credit line tailored to the business.
- Employee expense management: physical/virtual cards, custom limits, spend categories, real-time visibility, QuickBooks/Xero/NetSuite integrations.
- Global payments: send payments to 180+ countries, up to 32/34 currencies, 1% FX fee, USD international wires marketed as free.
- Bill Pay/AP automation: invoice upload/email ingestion, AI extraction, approvals, ACH/wire/card payment, accounting sync.
- AR automation: marketed as "AI that keeps your invoices on schedule."
- Pay by Card: pay vendors with credit even if vendors do not accept cards.
- Owner Intelligence / AI CFO: marketed as an agentic ERP layer for insights and decision support.
- Personal/private banking: coming soon/waitlist, aimed at high-net-worth owners.

## Founder and origin

Flex began as Flexbase, founded by Zaid Rahman and Hadi Solh. Early TechCrunch coverage described Flexbase as construction-industry payments/credit infrastructure, born from cash-flow pain in construction businesses. Rahman is described as a Thiel Fellow. The company later evolved from construction-specific financing into a broader finance platform for business owners.

TechCrunch's March 2025 coverage says Flex was officially formed in 2022 by CEO Zaid Rahman after evolving from a construction platform operating as Flexbase Technologies into fintech for business owners. Current company page timeline says:

- 2022: first Flex card swipe; first 5 employees.
- 2023: launched Flex Credit Card; hit $1M in transactions; acquired Ghost Financial; raised $120M debt and equity.
- 2024: launched Flex Banking, Global Payments, and B2B Payments.
- 2025: hit $1B in transactions; launched Bill Pay/AP Automation.

## Regulatory and partner structure

Flex is a financial technology company, not a bank. Public pages name:

- Thread Bank, Member FDIC: banking services and debit card issuer.
- Lead Bank: Flex Business Credit Card issuer.
- Visa Global Services Inc. / Currencycloud: international payment services / global payments terms.
- Deposit sweep program: Thread Bank places deposits at program banks; up to $3M FDIC coverage may be available if conditions are met.

Sources: [homepage footer](https://www.flex.one/), [business banking footer](https://www.flex.one/business-banking), [legal page](https://www.flex.one/legal), [Currencycloud terms](https://www.currencycloud.com/legal/terms/), [FDIC support article](https://support.flex.one/hc/en-us/articles/25652266905485-FDIC-Insurance).

## Customer segment

Flex now targets middle-market business owners, especially high-net-worth owner-operators with companies generating meaningful revenue and complex cash flow needs. Third-party coverage says target companies generate around $3M-$100M in revenue. Website copy is explicitly owner-oriented: "Most financial platforms are built for bookkeepers, Flex is built for you."

This is important. Flex is not only selling to accountants or finance teams. It is selling to the owner who wants business credit, banking, AP, spend control, global payments, and eventually personal finance/private banking in one place.

## Competitive read

Flex competes against:

- Brex / Ramp: card, spend, expense, bill pay.
- Mercury / Relay / Rho: business banking.
- Amex: premium business card and owner status.
- Bill.com / Melio: bill pay/AP.
- Wise / banks / Currencycloud-enabled fintechs: international transfers.
- Private banks: personal/owner wealth relationship.

Its wedge is Net-60 credit and owner relationship. Its expansion path is full owner financial OS.

## Strategic lesson for us

Flex shows that the "AI CFO stack" does not have to start with stablecoins. It can start with credit, card, banking, and AP automation. Stablecoins may improve global settlement later, but the customer pain is broader: cash flow, approvals, reconciliation, vendor payments, owner visibility, and fewer fragmented systems.
